Legal Affairs Request Review Criteria Reference
Source: Legal Affairs Request Management Platform PDF, Version 1 requirements

Use these criteria when editing mock PDF checklist demo data. The frontend AI demo should evaluate every criterion for each attached PDF, then mark only the criteria that appear satisfied as checked.

Core request/document criteria:
1. Document type identified
2. Parties correctly identified
3. Request category matches the document
4. Request description matches the attached document
5. Effective date identified
6. Expiry date or end date identified
7. Scope clearly defined
8. Key obligations summarized
9. Payment terms included, if relevant
10. Funding terms included, if relevant
11. Term and termination clauses included
12. Confidentiality clause included
13. Data protection clause included, if relevant
14. Intellectual property clause included, if relevant
15. Publication rights reviewed, if relevant
16. Liability and indemnity reviewed
17. Insurance requirements reviewed, if relevant
18. Governing law and jurisdiction reviewed
19. Signature authority confirmed
20. Internal approvals obtained or identified
21. Missing clauses or missing information identified
22. Unusual or high-risk terms highlighted
23. Compared against university-approved template or standard position
24. Similar past legal opinion or reviewed agreement considered
25. Reviewer questions for requester identified
26. Final approved response/document storage needed

Notes:
- AI output is draft support only.
- Legal Affairs reviewers can manually adjust the checklist.
- Requesters can see checklist progress but cannot edit it.
- Criteria may be marked checked when the PDF satisfies them; unchecked means needs review, missing, unclear, or not applicable but not confirmed yet.
